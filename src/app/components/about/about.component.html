About

I am a London-based software engineer and general technology enthusiast.

I have an interest and/or professional experience in a wide range of technologies, starting from my time as Honourary IT Lead of my Primary School, through to my over 10 years of experience as a professional software engineer across the charity, healthcare, financial and insurance sectors.

I primarily work in the C#/.NET/Windows space, including building & deploying applications, configuring servers, and maintaining connections to external systems. I am particularly keen on making sure applications are easily deployable (TeamCity, Octopus), reliable (Seq, Zabbix, Serilog, documented code reviews with pull requests), and supportable (Confluence, Jira Service Desk).

I have also worked in Kotlin (for TeamCity) and Python (for some client integrations and personal/side projects).

I've tried to list everything I've worked with on my StackShare account, but I'm sure I've missed some out. I'm always happy to learn something new if it is the right tool for the task (I learnt Python just because a library I wanted to use was better in Py than C#!), rather than trying to force something I already know into a workflow where it doesn't fit - feel free to check out my Pluralsight profile to see what I've been learning recently.

I have a full CV available by request - please connect with me on LinkedIn and I'll gladly forward it to you.

Outside of computing and I.T., I am a volunteer with St John Ambulance with whom I am trained to the Emergency Ambulance Crew standard, and have a Certificate in Emergency Response Ambulance Driving (allowing me to use "blue lights & sirens").

I enjoy a wide array of books, music, movies, and games (board and video), as well as running, swimming, walking, and cycling (ideally while also travelling).
